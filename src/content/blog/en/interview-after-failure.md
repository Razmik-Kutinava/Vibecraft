A strong dev friend + a **bad** product-company interview. The feeling: **wide** tech debt — not just one language.

## The trap

You want to **quit**: “I’m not an engineer.” But building is often **about** shipping value and **honesty**: what’s wrong, **where** the gap is, **how** to fix it.

## Where to look

- Not “study for a year blindly,” but **decompose**: networking, HTTP, databases, a project you can **explain**.

- Interviews aren’t only **book knowledge** — without **basics** and **structure**, it’s hard.

## Practice

Show **how you think**, how you use **AI**, how you **ship** a feature — that’s closer to real work than memorized definitions.

**Don’t despair** is cliché — but the next step should be **concrete**, not “become a senior overnight.”
