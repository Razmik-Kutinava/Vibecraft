In practice: **many applications**, **many rejections** — and job posts that feel **stuck in time**.

## What stands out

- “**Deep** PostgreSQL knowledge” — while a focused **Postgres** product can be stood up in **days** when the problem is clear.
- Hiring a **“senior”** while someone could **ship** with a lower title plus modern tooling — but the form says **senior only**.

## Takeaway

**Tools** and **velocity** have changed; **hiring templates** often haven’t.

If you’re **hiring**, ask what people **built** and how they **explain** architecture — not only the title.

If you’re **job-hunting**, close real **gaps** (basics, coherent picture, practice) — and don’t treat every **no** as a verdict: the market is noisy.

For MVP buyers, the parallel is: **speed** and **clarity** from a vendor beat a **grade** on paper.
